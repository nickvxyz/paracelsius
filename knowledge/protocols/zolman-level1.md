# Zolman Level 1 — Essential Prevention

Baseline: 94 years. Every factor below subtracts from this when suboptimal.

## The 17 Factors

### 1. Biomarkers (max penalty: -15 years)
Keep all 17 normal: FEV1, FVC (lung function), CGM (continuous glucose), CRP (inflammation), Cys-C (kidney), ACR (albumin-creatinine ratio), NT-proBNP (cardiac), HbA1c (long-term glucose), Insulin, Triglycerides, Lp(a) (genetic CVD risk), ALT/ALP (liver), RDW (red cell variation), Hemoglobin, WBC (immune), NLR (neutrophil-lymphocyte ratio).
**Evidence:** All always abnormal vs all normal = 15-year difference in mortality risk. Each marker reflects a major organ system.
**Recovery:** Most biomarkers respond to lifestyle within 3-12 months. HbA1c responds in 3 months. CRP in weeks. Lp(a) is largely genetic — limited recovery.

### 2. Smoking (max penalty: -10 years)
0 cigarettes vs 20/day = 10-year lifespan difference.
**Evidence:** CDC: smoking is the leading preventable cause of death. Cessation at any age adds years.
**Recovery:** Lung function improves within 2 weeks. CVD risk halves in 1 year. Cancer risk normalizes in 10-15 years. Full recovery possible.

### 3. Mental Health (max penalty: -10 years)
None vs severe depression/anxiety = up to 10 years.
**Evidence:** Chronic stress, depression increase cortisol, inflammation, CVD risk. Meta-analyses link severe mental illness to 10-20 year reduced lifespan.
**Recovery:** CBT, medication, stress management, social support. Partial to full recovery depending on severity.

### 4. Exercise (max penalty: -8 years)
Target: >6 hours moderate/vigorous per week (max 3 vigorous) + 6,000 steps/day + top 2% for age on Bruce Treadmill test.
**Evidence:** Large cohort (N~160,000): >7h/week moderate exercise = ~39% lower all-cause mortality. WHO: inactive people have 20-30% higher death risk.
**Recovery:** Benefits begin within weeks. VO2max improves in 6-8 weeks. Full cardiovascular adaptation in 6-12 months.

### 5. Caloric Restriction (max penalty: -5 years, men)
Target: 10-20% below RDA.
**Evidence:** CALERIE trial (2-year RCT): 12% CR improved insulin sensitivity, blood pressure, inflammation (TNF-α). Animal studies show consistent lifespan extension.
**Recovery:** Metabolic improvements within weeks of starting CR.

### 6. AHEI-2010 Diet (max penalty: -5 years)
Alternative Healthy Eating Index 2010: high vegetables, fruits, whole grains, nuts, legumes, omega-3, moderate alcohol. Low red/processed meat, sugar, sodium, trans fats.
**Evidence:** Highest vs lowest AHEI quintile = 0.5-2.2 extra CVD-free years. Mediterranean/AHEI diets cut all-cause mortality 20-30%.
**Recovery:** Dietary changes show biomarker improvements within 2-4 weeks. Full lipid response in 3 months.

### 7. BMI & Body Composition (max penalty: -5 years)
Target: BMI 18.5-22.5, body fat 15% (men) / 20% (women), waist < hips.
**Evidence:** BMI 18.5-22.5 associated with lowest mortality in large prospective studies. Obesity significantly raises all-cause death.
**Recovery:** Sustainable weight loss of 0.5-1 kg/week through caloric deficit. Body composition changes visible in 8-12 weeks.

### 8. ApoB Levels (max penalty: -3 to -6 years)
Target: 20-60 mg/dL vs population average ~110 mg/dL.
**Evidence:** Lower ApoB = lower atherogenic particle count = reduced CVD. Each 1 mmol/L LDL reduction cuts CVD risk ~22%.
**Recovery:** Diet (fiber, plant sterols) can reduce ApoB 10-15%. Statins reduce 30-50%. Aggressive targets may require medication (physician decision).

### 9. Blood Pressure (max penalty: -3 years)
Target: under 115/70 vs 140/80.
**Evidence:** Each 10 mmHg systolic reduction cuts CVD risk ~25%. Meta-analyses of millions of participants.
**Recovery:** Exercise, salt reduction, weight loss can lower BP 5-15 mmHg in weeks. Medication if needed.

### 10. Hormones (max penalty: -2 to -5 years)
Men: Free testosterone 0.4-0.6 nmol/L (115-170 ng/dL USA). Women: HRT post-menopause.
**Evidence:** Optimal sex hormones linked to muscle mass, bone density, cognitive function, CVD risk. Testosterone replacement in deficient men improves metabolic markers. HRT in early menopause reduces CVD and osteoporosis.
**Recovery:** Lifestyle (exercise, sleep, weight management) can improve testosterone 10-20%. HRT is a medical decision.

### 11. Guideline Screening (max penalty: -2 years)
Follow recommended screening: cancer (colonoscopy, mammography, PSA as appropriate), diabetes (HbA1c), cardiovascular (lipids, BP), eye exams, dental.
**Evidence:** Early detection of cancer, diabetes, CVD saves years through timely treatment.
**Recovery:** Immediate — just schedule the tests.

### 12. Sleep (max penalty: -2 to -5 years)
Meet 5 criteria: 7-8 hours, consistent schedule, high efficiency, minimal awakenings, good subjective quality.
**Evidence:** Meta-analysis (46 studies): short sleep (<7h) raises mortality 14%. Chronic poor sleep linked to obesity, diabetes, CVD, cognitive decline, weakened immunity.
**Recovery:** Sleep hygiene improvements show results in 1-2 weeks. Full circadian reset in 2-4 weeks.

### 13. Vitamins (max penalty: -2 years)
Vitamin D: 75-125 nmol/L (30-50 ng/mL). B12 and folate: middle of reference range.
**Evidence:** Vitamin D deficiency linked to bone loss, immune dysfunction, increased mortality. B12/folate deficiency causes neurological damage and anemia.
**Recovery:** Supplementation corrects levels within 4-8 weeks.

### 14. Social Strength (max penalty: -2 years)
Strong bonds and purpose vs isolated and purposeless.
**Evidence:** Meta-analysis (148 studies, N~308,000): strong social relationships = 50% higher survival odds. Effect size rivals smoking cessation.
**Recovery:** Building social connections is gradual. Join groups, volunteer, maintain friendships. Effects on health markers within months.

### 15. Air Quality (max penalty: -1 year)
Perfect air vs 12 µg/m³ PM2.5 average exposure.
**Evidence:** WHO estimates ~7 million premature deaths/year from air pollution. PM2.5 increases CVD, respiratory disease, cancer.
**Recovery:** Air purifiers, location changes, avoiding pollution peaks. Lung inflammation reduces within weeks of cleaner air.

### 16. Oral Health (max penalty: -1 year)
Perfect oral health vs chronic periodontal disease.
**Evidence:** Gum disease linked to systemic inflammation, endocarditis, CVD. Chronic oral infections elevate CRP.
**Recovery:** Professional dental care + daily hygiene. Inflammation reduces within weeks of treatment.

### 17. Alcohol (max penalty: -0.58 years / ~7 months)
Reducing from 7 drinks/week to 1. Higher penalty if family history of cancer.
**Evidence:** Even moderate alcohol raises cancer risk (breast, liver, GI). No safe level for cancer prevention. Cardiovascular "benefit" of moderate drinking now disputed.
**Recovery:** Liver recovery begins within days of reducing intake. Full metabolic benefits in 1-3 months.

## Total Theoretical Maximum
94 - sum(all penalties) = projected lifespan.
Maximum total penalty: ~85.58 years (theoretical worst case: ~8.4 years).
Realistic range for average person: 60-80 years projected.
