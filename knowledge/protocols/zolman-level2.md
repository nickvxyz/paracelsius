# Zolman Level 2 — Non-Age-Related Factors

Level 2 addresses quality of life and personalized risk management beyond the 17 core factors. These don't directly target aging biology but improve overall health and resilience.

## 12 Categories

### 1. Healthcare Team & Support
Build a team: primary care physician, specialists as needed (cardiologist, endocrinologist), therapist, nutritionist, fitness coach. Supportive friends and family.
**Why it matters:** Continuity of care improves outcomes. People who see the same doctor regularly have better chronic disease management and earlier detection.

### 2. Chronic & Rare Conditions
Identify and manage any existing conditions: autoimmune diseases, diabetes, heart disease, thyroid disorders, rare genetic conditions.
**Action:** Get a comprehensive diagnosis. Follow evidence-based treatment protocols. Don't let unmanaged conditions silently erode your baseline.

### 3. Sexual Health
Optimize reproductive and sexual function. HPV vaccination, STI prevention, treatment of dysfunction.
**For men:** Monitor prostate health, testosterone levels.
**For women:** Menstrual cycle-aware nutrition, menopause management (HRT when appropriate), bone density monitoring.

### 4. MSK Injury Prevention
Musculoskeletal health: proper training form, warm-ups, mobility work, physiotherapy for chronic issues. Balance training for fall prevention in older adults.
**Why:** A broken hip at 70 can cut years. Prevention is cheap.

### 5. Avoiding Harm
Avoid unproven therapies, quack practitioners, dangerous supplements, high-risk environments. Evidence-based medicine only.
**Paracelsus note:** "In my era, physicians killed more individuals than the plague. Insist on evidence."

### 6. Evidence-Based Medicine Skills
Learn to evaluate health claims. Understand study design (RCT vs observational), effect sizes, conflict of interest. Use guideline-based calculators for personalized screening.
**Why:** Health literacy prevents both under-treatment and over-treatment.

### 7. Genomics
Consider genetic testing for actionable variants: BRCA (breast/ovarian cancer), APOE4 (Alzheimer's risk), familial hypercholesterolemia, pharmacogenomics (drug metabolism).
**Limitations:** Most polygenic risk scores have limited predictive power beyond traditional risk factors. Don't over-interpret consumer genomics.

### 8. Cognitive Enhancement
Maintain brain health: lifelong learning, memory exercises, social engagement, adequate sleep, physical exercise (which is the strongest neuroprotective intervention).
**Evidence:** Cognitive engagement and exercise reduce dementia risk. No supplement has strong evidence for cognitive enhancement in healthy people.

### 9. Personal Health Devices
Wearable trackers (sleep, HR, HRV, steps), home blood pressure monitors, continuous glucose monitors, smart scales.
**Why:** Self-monitoring increases awareness and adherence. Home BP monitoring reduces hypertension by catching it early.

### 10. Environmental Exposures
Air quality (purifiers, avoiding pollution), water quality, light exposure (morning sunlight for circadian rhythm, blue light reduction at night), noise reduction.
**Evidence:** WHO: ~7 million premature deaths/year from air pollution. Clean environments reduce respiratory, cardiovascular, and cancer mortality.

### 11. Emergency Medicine & Preparedness
Learn CPR, first aid. Keep an AED at home if at risk. Know your blood type, allergies, medications. Have emergency contacts accessible.
**Why:** Survival from cardiac arrest doubles with bystander CPR.

### 12. Biobanking
Store personal genetic data, blood samples, or tissue for future use. Largely experimental.
**Current value:** Limited. May become valuable as personalized medicine advances.

## When to Recommend Level 2
- After Level 1 basics are stable (user is sleeping well, exercising, eating right)
- When user has specific conditions or risks (genetic, chronic disease)
- When user asks about optimization beyond the 17 factors
- Older adults benefit more from Level 2 (fall prevention, screening, healthcare team)
