# Evidence Grades

## Grading Scale
- **A — Strong:** Multiple large RCTs or meta-analyses. Consistent results. Guidelines recommend.
- **B — Moderate:** Some RCTs, large cohort studies. Consistent direction but gaps.
- **C — Emerging:** Small trials, animal studies, mechanistic plausibility. Not yet proven in humans.
- **D — Speculative:** Theoretical, anecdotal, or preclinical only.

## Level 1 Factors — Evidence Grades

| Factor | Grade | Key Evidence |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| Smoking cessation | A | CDC/WHO: leading preventable cause of death |
| Exercise (≥150 min/wk) | A | N~160,000 cohort: 39% lower mortality at >7h/week |
| Diet quality (AHEI) | A | Meta-analyses: 20-30% lower all-cause mortality |
| Sleep (7-8h) | A | 46-study meta: <7h = 14% higher mortality |
| Social connections | A | 148 studies: 50% higher survival with strong bonds |
| Blood pressure <120/80 | A | Each 10 mmHg reduction = 25% lower CVD risk |
| BMI 18.5-22.5 | A | Prospective Studies Collaboration: lowest mortality |
| Biomarkers (17 normal) | B | Individual markers well-validated; combined effect extrapolated |
| Mental health | B | Meta-analyses link severe illness to 10-20yr reduction |
| ApoB / cholesterol | B | Lower ApoB = lower CVD; aggressive targets extrapolated |
| Caloric restriction | B | CALERIE trial: metabolic benefits; lifespan data limited in humans |
| Vitamins D, B12, folate | B | Deficiency correction is strong; supplementation in adequate levels unclear |
| Oral health | B | Periodontal disease linked to CVD; magnitude uncertain |
| Air quality | B | WHO: 7M deaths/year from pollution |
| Alcohol reduction | B | Cancer risk increase even at moderate levels; CV "benefit" disputed |
| Hormones (T/E) | B-C | Replacement in deficiency helps; optimal levels for longevity uncertain |
| Screening | B | Cancer/diabetes screening reduces mortality in at-risk groups |

## Level 2 — Evidence Grades
| Category | Grade | Notes |
|----------|-------|-------|
| Healthcare team | B | Continuity of care improves outcomes |
| Chronic disease management | A | Following guidelines is well-supported |
| Genomics | C | Actionable for some variants; most polygenic scores limited |
| Cognitive enhancement | C | Exercise is strongest; supplements unproven in healthy adults |
| Wearables | B | Home BP monitoring reduces hypertension; activity trackers increase movement |

## Level 3 — Evidence Grades
All Level 3 interventions are **C or D** (experimental, preclinical, or early trial).

## How Paracelsus Uses Evidence Grades
- When citing a recommendation, mentally check the grade.
- A/B grade: state with confidence. "The evidence is robust."
- C grade: state with qualification. "Promising, but not yet proven in humans."
- D grade: state with caution. "Theoretical. I mention it for completeness, not as a recommendation."
- Never present C/D evidence as if it were A.
